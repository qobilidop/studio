---

# gemini response

**[March 21, 2026, 9:46 PM PDT]**

"ALT MODE" stands for **DisplayPort Alternate Mode**. In the context of docking stations and USB-C, it means that specific port uses your computer's native graphics card (GPU) to send a raw, uncompressed video signal directly through the cable. 

Here is exactly why this matters for your specific setup with an M1 MacBook Pro and a triple-monitor dock:

**1. The Base M1 Limitation**
The base Apple M1 chip has a strict hardware limitation: it only natively supports **one** external monitor. 

**2. How the Dock Bypasses This (The Hybrid Approach)**
Docks like the Wavlink you linked use a hybrid approach to give you three screens:
* **The "ALT MODE" Port:** This port will use up your M1's single native display allowance. It draws a direct, uncompressed signal from the Mac's GPU.
* **The Other Two Ports (DisplayLink):** These ports don't use the Mac's native video output. Instead, they rely on a built-in DisplayLink chip inside the dock. You install the DisplayLink Manager software on your Mac, which compresses your video output into standard USB data. The dock then receives that USB data and translates it back into video for the second and third monitors. 

**3. How to Arrange Your Setup**
Because DisplayLink relies on software compression, it can have a tiny bit of latency and uses a small amount of CPU overhead. Alt Mode has zero software latency. 

When you connect everything, you'll want to be strategic about which monitor plugs into which port. Your terminals, VS Code, and browser windows are perfectly suited for the DisplayLink ports since static text and standard coding workflows aren't affected by slight compression. However, when you drop into Teyvat to clear out some quests, you'll absolutely want your main entertainment screen plugged into that HDMI ALT MODE port to ensure you get the lowest latency and smoothest frame rates possible. 

Just remember that for the two DisplayLink ports to light up at all, you will need to download and run the free DisplayLink Manager app on your Mac. The Alt Mode port will work plug-and-play.

# gemini response

**[March 21, 2026, 10:52 PM PDT]**

Plugable actually doesn't have its own proprietary DisplayLink app. The software you see on their website is the exact same **Synaptics DisplayLink Manager**.

Synaptics is the company that invented and owns the DisplayLink technology, meaning they manufacture the actual microchips inside the docks and write the software drivers. Brands like Plugable, Anker, and Wavlink buy those chips to build their physical hardware. Plugable just hosts the app on their own website purely for convenience and to pair it with their own customer support documentation.

You can absolutely download and use the official app directly from the Synaptics website. Whether you get it from Synaptics or Plugable, you are installing the exact same program. 

When you install it on your M1 MacBook Pro, there is one critical step to keep in mind: macOS will prompt you to grant the app **Screen Recording permissions**. It sounds a bit alarming, but the software isn't actually recording or saving anything. It simply needs that system-level permission to "see" your video output so it can compress it into USB data and send it to your extra monitors.
