# gemini response

**Current Timestamp:** March 25, 2026, 10:58 PM PDT

"Employer work" and "corporate work" definitely get the point across, but they can feel a bit sterile. Depending on whether you're using this as a quick tag for your daily log or as a directory name in your knowledge repository, you might want something that flows a bit better or specifically highlights the confidentiality constraint.

Here are a few angles you could take:

**Focusing on Confidentiality**
* **Proprietary:** Sounds professional and clearly establishes that the IP belongs to someone else and cannot be shared. 
* **Closed-Source:** A great nod to software development that perfectly contrasts with any open-source projects you might be working on.
* **Confidential:** Simple, direct, and leaves zero ambiguity about why it's not being discussed in detail.

**Focusing on the Professional/Financial Aspect**
* **Commercial:** Highlights that it's paid, industry work without necessarily implying a massive "corporate" structure.
* **Primary Contract:** Sounds formal and agentic, fitting well if you view your time as a resource you're allocating.
* **Professional Duties:** A bit formal, but very clean. 

**Casual & Classic**
* **Day Job:** Honestly, keeping it simple with "Day Job" (or `day-job` for a folder name) is universally understood and relatable. 
* **The Grind:** A bit more informal, but highly accurate for the daily block of time dedicated to the employer.

"Closed-Source" or "Proprietary" usually work best if you need to draw a hard line between what you can and can't share publicly. 

Do any of these hit the right note for your logs, or would you like to brainstorm a few more in a specific direction?
